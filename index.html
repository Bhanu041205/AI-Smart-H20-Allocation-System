01 — Problem Statement

Why existing systems fail

Water is essential — but management today is reactive, siloed, and unable to anticipate the crises of tomorrow.

Background

The Global Water Crisis

  • Rapid population growth increases water demand yearly
  • Climate change disrupts rainfall patterns unpredictably
  • Urbanization accelerates reservoir depletion
  • Decisions rely on historical data, not predictive intelligence
  • No early warning systems for drought conditions exist
Core Problems

What Existing Systems Cannot Do

  • Predict future water demand accurately
  • Anticipate drought conditions early enough
  • Dynamically optimize water allocation in real time
  • Integrate multiple data sources into one platform
  • Generate automated alerts for critical thresholds
Objective

What This Platform Achieves

  • Forecast future water demand using LSTM deep learning
  • Predict drought risk levels via XGBoost classification
  • Optimize sector-wise allocation using PuLP Linear Programming
  • Generate intelligent threshold alerts automatically
  • Provide a real-time React dashboard for decision-makers
Expected Outcome

Impact for Water Authorities

  • Accurate multi-step water demand forecasting
  • Early drought detection before crises occur
  • Optimal, mathematically justified sector allocation
  • Significant reduction in water wastage
  • A decision-support tool accessible to non-experts

02 — Literature Survey

5 papers studied and understood

Spanning demand forecasting, IoT monitoring, drought prediction, reservoir optimization, and decision-support systems.

PAPER 01

ML Approaches for Water Demand Forecasting

Smith et al. · 2021

  • Compared Linear Regression, Random Forest, and ANN
  • ML outperforms traditional statistical methods
  • Validated RF accuracy for non-linear demand patterns

Limitation: Focused only on domestic water — no sector-wise allocation or drought prediction component.

PAPER 02

Smart Water Management Using IoT and AI

Kumar & Rao · 2022

  • Integrated IoT sensors with AI for real-time monitoring
  • Reduced leakage and wastage through smart tracking
  • Validated AI usefulness in live water distribution

Limitation: No future demand forecasting. No intelligent allocation recommendations whatsoever.

PAPER 03

Rainfall Prediction & Drought Assessment Using XGBoost

Chen et al. · 2020

  • XGBoost for environmental classification tasks
  • Achieved high accuracy in rainfall and drought prediction
  • Directly validates our XGBoost drought risk module

Limitation: Drought prediction only — did not address water demand forecasting or allocation.

PAPER 04

Optimization Techniques for Reservoir Water Allocation

Patel & Singh · 2019

  • Mathematical optimization for reservoir operation
  • Introduced sector-priority-based allocation strategies
  • Foundational logic for our PuLP allocation engine

Limitation: Static allocation rules with no ML prediction — cannot adapt to future demand.

PAPER 05 · Most Relevant

AI Decision Support Systems for Sustainable Water Management

Lee et al. · 2023

  • Combined ML with visualization dashboards for planning
  • Enhanced long-term decision-making for water authorities
  • Closest in vision — directly informs our dashboard design

Limitation: Did not integrate weather + reservoir + population + consumption data together. No end-to-end allocation system — exactly the gap this project fills.

Why these three models?

Step 04 · Deep Learning

🧠 LSTM

  • Water demand is inherently time-series
  • Captures long-term seasonal patterns
  • Learns multi-variable temporal dependencies
  • Outperforms regression on sequential data
  • Handles variable-length input sequences
Step 05 · Classification

⚡ XGBoost

  • Drought risk is a classification task
  • Best-in-class on tabular environmental data
  • Built-in handling for class imbalance
  • Fast, scalable gradient boosting
  • Interpretable feature importance scores
Step 06 · Optimization

⚖️ PuLP (LP)

  • Allocation is an optimization, not prediction
  • Mathematically guaranteed optimal output
  • Easily encodes priority constraints
  • Integrates drought risk as a constraint
  • Transparent and auditable solution
